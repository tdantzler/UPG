**UPG Benefits**

AI & Workflow Automation --- Strategy Brief

*Discovery prep, high-impact ideas, and walk-in framing*

Overview & Objective

This document outlines a strategy for introducing AI and workflow
automation to UPG Benefits. It is structured as a pre-discovery brief
--- designed to help frame the first conversation, ask the right
questions, and walk in with concrete, credible ideas that demonstrate
immediate value.

The goals of the engagement are:

-   Surface the specific operational pain points where AI and automation
    create the most leverage

-   Demonstrate capability through live demos and tangible concepts in
    the first meeting

-   Position the engagement as a strategic partnership, not a technology
    sale

-   Leave with a clear next step: a workflow audit and prioritized
    roadmap

Industry Context --- Why Benefits Brokers Need This Now

Benefits brokerages operate in a high-touch, document-heavy,
compliance-sensitive environment. The core business model --- advising
employers on plan selection, managing renewals, supporting employees
during enrollment --- is highly manual and relationship-driven. This
creates both significant pain and significant opportunity for AI.

Key industry dynamics that create urgency:

-   **---** Renewal season is universally chaotic

Most brokers manage dozens of simultaneous renewals with the same team.
The process --- pulling carrier quotes, generating comparison decks,
drafting client communications --- is heavily manual and prone to error.

-   **---** Employee questions never stop

HR teams and brokers field a constant stream of \'what does my plan
cover?\' questions. These are time-consuming to answer manually and
rarely require a human expert --- a well-trained AI can handle 80%+ of
them instantly.

-   **---** Open enrollment is crisis season

Every fall, brokers scramble to support clients through a compressed
window of decisions. The volume of employee confusion, plan comparison
requests, and administrative work spikes dramatically.

-   **---** Competition is increasing

Larger brokerages and benefits platforms are investing in technology.
Independent brokers who do not adopt AI tools will struggle to match the
service quality and capacity of better-resourced competitors.

-   **---** Compliance demands are growing

ACA, ERISA, and state-level regulatory changes require constant
monitoring and client communication. Most brokers track this manually.

High-Impact AI & Automation Ideas

These are the concepts to lead with --- either as live demos, concrete
proposals, or \'imagine if\' conversations. Prioritized by immediacy of
impact and ease of demonstration.

Immediate Wow Moments --- Show These in the Meeting

1\. Live Benefits Q&A AI

An AI that answers \'what does my plan cover?\' questions instantly by
reading their actual plan documents.

-   **Setup:** How it works:

Feed the AI their benefits summary PDFs. It reads and understands the
documents. Employees or HR can then ask questions in plain English and
get accurate, sourced answers instantly.

-   **In the meeting:** Demo play:

Ask them to bring a benefits PDF. Paste it into a live AI tool in real
time and start asking questions. Show it returning accurate answers from
their own content. Nothing lands like seeing it work on your own
documents.

-   High ROI --- reduces call volume to HR and the broker

-   Fast to build --- a working prototype can be demo-ready in days

-   Immediately understandable --- no technical explanation required

2\. Enrollment AI Assistant

An AI that guides employees through open enrollment decisions ---
comparing plans side by side, explaining tradeoffs in plain English, and
making a recommendation based on the employee\'s situation.

-   Dramatically reduces broker and HR bandwidth during peak enrollment
    season

-   Improves employee satisfaction --- most employees find open
    enrollment confusing

-   Can be white-labeled and offered as a client-facing service
    differentiator

-   Build timeline: approximately 6 to 8 weeks for a full working
    version

Core Workflow Automation --- The Foundational Plays

3\. Renewal Automation Engine

Automate the annual renewal cycle --- the most painful recurring process
for most brokerages.

Current state at most brokers: account managers spend weeks pulling
carrier quotes, building comparison spreadsheets, formatting decks, and
drafting client-facing communications --- all manually, all under time
pressure.

Future state with automation:

-   Carrier data flows in via API or structured upload

-   AI generates comparison decks automatically, flagging rate changes
    and coverage shifts

-   Draft client communication is generated and queued for broker review

-   Renewal timeline and task tracking is automated with client-specific
    reminders

A 3-week scramble becomes a smooth, supervised process. Brokers shift
from doing the work to reviewing and personalizing it.

4\. Client Intelligence Dashboard

Surface the signals that tell brokers which clients need attention
before those clients call --- or leave.

What the AI monitors and surfaces:

-   Utilization trends --- which clients are over- or under-utilizing
    their plans

-   Rate shock predictions --- which clients face significant premium
    increases at renewal

-   Engagement gaps --- which clients have not heard from the broker
    recently

-   Compliance exposure --- which clients may have regulatory issues
    coming

The broker sees a prioritized list of clients who need action. AI
surfaces the insight; the broker makes the call. This is a retention
play and a relationship deepening tool.

Strategic Plays --- Competitive Differentiation

5\. Proposal & RFP Generator

AI drafts full client proposals from a brief --- pulling in plan data,
carrier comparisons, and pricing narrative. The broker reviews,
personalizes, and sends. Proposal time drops from days to hours.

6\. Compliance Monitoring & Client Advisories

Track ACA, ERISA, and state-level regulatory changes automatically. AI
identifies which clients are affected by a given change and drafts a
personalized advisory notice for broker review. Turns a liability into a
proactive value-add service.

7\. Client Onboarding Automation

From signed agreement to fully onboarded client --- automated document
collection, carrier submissions, benefits guide generation, welcome
packets, and first check-in scheduling. Zero manual handoffs. The
account manager supervises rather than executes.

8\. Claims Triage & Advocacy

AI helps employees navigate denied claims --- analyzing Explanations of
Benefits (EOBs), identifying grounds for appeal, drafting appeal
letters, and routing to the right contacts. A meaningful differentiator
versus brokers who simply refer clients to carrier 800 numbers.

Walk-In Framing --- How to Run the First Meeting

1\. Open with their world, not your product

Lead with what you already know about benefits brokers --- renewal
season chaos, employee Q&A volume, the proposal grind. Show you
understand their world before pitching anything.

Opening line to consider:

> *\"We work with companies like yours and we keep hearing the same
> things. Does any of this sound familiar?\"*

2\. Reframe AI as a staffing and growth decision

Every hour your brokers spend on admin is an hour not spent on
relationships and revenue. AI does not replace the broker --- it removes
the tax on their time.

The reframe:

> *\"What if your team could handle 2x the book of business with the
> same headcount, and deliver better service doing it?\"*

3\. Run the live demo --- ask for a PDF in advance

One of the most powerful moves in the first meeting: ask them to bring a
benefits summary PDF. Then, in the meeting, paste it into a live AI tool
and let it answer real employee questions from their actual content.

Nothing sells AI like seeing it work on your own documents. A good demo
does more than 30 minutes of explanation.

Pre-meeting ask:

-   Email them before the meeting: \"If you have a benefits summary PDF
    handy, bring it --- we want to show you something that will take 90
    seconds and be worth the trip.\"

4\. Handle the \'we\'re not ready for AI\' objection

Do not let \'we\'re not a tech company\' kill the conversation. Reframe:

You are not asking them to transform overnight. Start with one workflow
--- say, employee benefits Q&A or renewal document generation --- prove
the value in 30 days, then expand. Low risk, high proof.

5\. Leave with a workflow audit as the next step

Do not leave selling a product. Leave offering a workflow audit:

> *\"Let us spend 2 hours with your team mapping where time is being
> lost. We\'ll come back with a prioritized roadmap of what AI can
> solve, in what order, and at what cost.\"*

This is low-commitment and high-value. It positions you as a strategic
partner, not a vendor. It is also the cleanest possible close --- it is
hard to say no to a free audit.

Pre-Meeting Research Checklist

Gather these before the first meeting to sharpen every conversation:

Their tech stack

-   Are they in a BenAdmin platform? Common ones: Employee Navigator,
    Ease, Benefitfocus, PlanSource, bswift

-   What CRM do they use, if any?

-   Do they have any carrier data integrations, or is everything manual
    / portal-based?

Their business profile

-   How many employer clients do they manage?

-   How large is the team --- account managers, service reps, sales?

-   What market segments do they focus on --- small group, mid-market,
    large group?

-   Any specialty lines --- dental, vision, voluntary, ancillary?

Their competitive position

-   Who are their main competitors in their market?

-   What do they advertise as their differentiator today?

-   Are they growing, stable, or under pressure?
